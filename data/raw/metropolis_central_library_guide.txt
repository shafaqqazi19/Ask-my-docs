====================================================================
THE METROPOLIS CENTRAL LIBRARY - INFORMATION & VISITOR GUIDE
====================================================================

Welcome to the Metropolis Central Library, a sanctuary for lifelong 
learning, community engagement, and cultural preservation. Founded in 
1892, our library serves over 500,000 residents, housing a collection 
of more than 1.2 million physical and digital items.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
1. HOURS OF OPERATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* Monday – Thursday: 9:00 AM – 9:00 PM
* Friday – Saturday: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
* Sunday: 1:00 PM – 5:00 PM
(Note: Closed on all major public holidays)

--------------------------------------------------------------------
2. FLOOR PLAN & SECTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Level 1: Main Lobby & Customer Service
- Circulation Desk (Check-outs, returns, and library cards)
- New Arrivals & Bestsellers Display
- The Café & Social Commons
- Children's Discovery Wing (Ages 0-12)

Level 2: Fiction & Multimedia
- General Fiction, Mystery, Sci-Fi, and Graphic Novels
- Audiobooks, Blu-rays, and Music CDs
- Teen Space (Ages 13-18) with study pods and gaming stations
- Digital Media Lab (3D printing, video editing suites)

Level 3: Non-Fiction & Reference
- History, Science, Philosophy, Arts, and Biography collections
- Periodicals Reading Room (Current newspapers and magazines)
- Quiet Study Zone & Individual Carrels
- Public Computer Lab (50 high-speed internet terminals)

Level 4: Local History & Special Collections
- The Metropolis Historical Archive (Newspapers dating back to 1875)
- Genealogy Research Center
- Rare Books Vault (Access by appointment only)
- Administrative Offices

--------------------------------------------------------------------
3. DIGITAL RESOURCES
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Cardholders enjoy 24/7 free access to our digital branch:
- OverDrive / Libby: Download thousands of eBooks and audiobooks.
- Kanopy: Stream award-winning documentaries and indie films.
- JSTOR & EBSCOhost: Access academic journals and research databases.
- Mango Languages: Interactive courses for over 70 foreign languages.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
4. COMMUNITY PROGRAMS & SERVICES
--------------------------------------------------------------------
* Weekly Children's Storytime: Every Tuesday and Thursday at 10:30 AM.
* Tech Help Desk: One-on-one troubleshooting assistance for devices 
  and software (Saturdays 2:00 PM - 4:00 PM).
* Meeting Room Bookings: Free reservations for local non-profit 
  organizations and study groups.
* Interlibrary Loan (ILL): If we don't have a book, we will borrow it 
  from another library system for you at no cost.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
5. LIBRARY POLICIES & BORROWING RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------
- Borrowing Limits: Up to 30 items total at one time.
- Loan Periods: 
  * Books & Audiobooks: 3 weeks
  * Express Bestsellers & DVDs: 1 week
- Renewals: Items will automatically renew up to 3 times unless 
  another patron has placed a hold on them.
- Code of Conduct: Please respect your fellow visitors. Keep 
  conversations at a low volume on Levels 1 and 2; Level 3 is strictly 
  a silent study area. Covered beverages are permitted; hot or open 
  food must be consumed in the Level 1 Café area.

====================================================================
Thank you for supporting your local library. Knowledge is free here!
